MAN SENTENCED TO NINE YEARS IN PRISON FOR ATTACKING HIS WIFE WITH MEAT CLEAVER AFTER ARGUMENT IN GARDEN GROVE APARTMENT
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A man was sentenced to nine years in state prison today for attacking his wife with a meat cleaver after an argument in their Garden Grove aparctment.
Defendant
Charges
Sentence
Thang Van Nguyen, 48, Garden Grove
Found guilty by a jury on Feb. 9, 2018, of the following felony counts:
Attempted voluntary manslaughter
Mayhem
Corporal injury on spouse/cohabitant
Sentencing Enhancements Found True:
(2) Domestic violence causing great bodily injury
(3) Personal use of a deadly weapon
Nine years in state prison
Circumstances of the Case
At the time of the crime, Nguyen and Jane Doe lived with their children in an apartment in Garden Grove.
At approximately 6:45 a.m. on Jan. 19, 2016, Nguyen verbally argued with Jane Doe in their apartment. During the course of the argument, Nguyen walked into the kitchen and grabbed a meat cleaver and attacked the victim. The defendant swung the meat cleaver at Jane Doe’s head and left her severely wounded with deep lacerations.
The children were in the residence at the time of the crime, but were not harmed.
Nguyen contacted the Garden Grove Fire Department and paramedics transported the victim to the University of California, Irvine Medical Center, where she was placed in a medically induced coma and treated for a brain injury.
The Garden Grove Police Department investigated this case.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Mark Geller, Family Protection Unit
