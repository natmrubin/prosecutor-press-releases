MAN CONVICTED OF VEHICULAR MANSLAUGHTER AND CAUSING GREAT BODILY INJURY WHILE DRIVING UNDER THE INFLUENCE OF ALCOHOL
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A man was convicted yesterday of vehicular manslaughter and causing great bodily injury while driving under the influence of alcohol.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Steven Gerard Cruz, 34, Cerritos
Found guilty by a jury Aug. 28, 2018 on the following felony counts:
Gross vehicular manslaughter while intoxicated
Driving under the influence of alcohol causing injury
Driving with blood alcohol .08 percent or more causing bodily injury
Sentencing Enhancements:
(2) Great bodily injury
12 years and eight months in state prison
Sentencing
Sept. 20, 2018, 1:30 p.m.
Department C-36, Central Justice Center, Santa Ana
Circumstances of the Case
On May 10, 2015, Cruz was driving east on Orangethorpe Avenue in Buena Park while under the influence of alcohol with a blood alcohol content of .08 percent or more.
At an intersection, the defendant accelerated through a yellow light and crashed into the driver’s side of a Toyota Avalon.
John Doe, the driver of the Toyota Avalon, was transported by ambulance to UCI Medical Center where he later died of his injuries.
Cruz also caused great bodily injury to Jane Doe 1 and Jane Doe 2, who were John Doe’s passengers.
Buena Park Police Department (BPPD), who investigated this case, found that the defendant had a blood alcohol content of 0.11 percent at the scene.
Prosecutor: Senior Deputy District Attorney Daniel Feldman, Homicide Unit
