TWO MEN ARRAIGNED FOR ROBBING AND BURGLARIZING RESIDENCE IN SEAL BEACH WHILE VICTIMS WERE HOME
SANTA ANA – Two men were arraigned today for robbing and burglarizing a residence in Seal Beach while the victims were home.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Aaron Michael Lipsey, 34, Costa Mesa
Richard Henry Derderian, 33, Costa Mesa
Charged on Aug. 31, 2017, with the following felony counts:
(3) First degree robbery
(1) First degree burglary
(3) Dissuade witness by force or threat
(3) Criminal threats
11 years, eight months in state prison
Pre-trial hearing
Sept. 12, 2017, 8:30 a.m.
Department W-2, West Justice Center, Westminster
Circumstances of the Case
On Aug. 21, 2017, Lipsey and Derderian are accused of entering a residence in the city of Seal Beach while three victims were inside the home.
The defendants are further accused of ransacking the residence, removing a safe belonging to one of the victims, and robbing each occupant of the home.
Before fleeing the scene, the defendants are accused of threatening to harm the victims if they called the police.
At approximately 4:30 p.m., officers from Seal Beach Police Department (SBPD) arrived to the scene and began investigating this case.
SBPD arrested the defendants on Aug. 30, 2017.
Senior Deputy District Attorney Jason Baez of the Special Prosecutions Unit is prosecuting this case.
