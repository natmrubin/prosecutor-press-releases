LAW ENFORCEMENT SEEKING POTENTIAL ADDITIONAL VICTIMS OF DOCTOR CHARGED WITH SENDING SEXUALLY MOTIVATED MESSAGES TO HIGH SCHOOL CHEERLEADERS
SANTA ANA, Calif. – Law enforcement is seeking potential additional victims of a doctor who is charged with sending sexually motivated messages to cheerleaders at a high school where he served as a volunteer.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
David Lee Haller, 51, Newport Beach
Charged on June 18, 2018, with the following misdemeanor counts:
(2) Child annoyance
Two years in county jail
Arraignment
July 25, 2018, 8:30 a.m.
Department H-8, Harbor Justice Center, Newport Beach
Circumstances of the Case
At the time of the crime, Haller was a medical doctor and a volunteer with the University of California, Irvine (UCI) Spirit Squad cheerleading team. The defendant also volunteered as a doctor at several athletic events at Newport Harbor High School (NHHS) from October to December 2017.
On Aug. 30, 2017, Haller is accused of messaging NHHS cheerleader Jane Doe 1 on Instagram in Jane Doe 1 on Instagram in a sexually motivated and inappropriate manner.
On Oct. 27, 2017, the defendant is accused of contacting NHHS cheerleader Jane Doe 2 on Snapchat in a similar sexually motivated and inappropriate manner.
The victims contacted the Newport Beach Police Department (NBPD), who investigated this case and identified Haller with assistance from the UCI Police Department.
Anyone with additional information or who believes they are a victim is asked to contact Orange County District Attorney’s Office Supervising Investigator Paul Carvo at 714-834-5025.
