Case # 18NM07368
Date: May 15, 2018
MASKED PROTESTOR CHARGED WITH ILLEGALLY POSSESSING TEAR GAS AT CALIFORNIA STATE UNIVERISTY, FULLERTON MILO YIANNOPOULOS
SPEAKING EVENT
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A masked protestor was charged with illegally possessing tear gas outside of a Milo Yiannopoulos speaking event at California State University, Fullerton (CSUF).
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Rhapsody Elle Johnson, 24, Los Angeles
Charged on May 15, 2018, with:
One misdemeanor count of felon in possession of tear gas
One year in county jail
Arraignment
June 12, 2018, 8:30 a.m.
Department N-8, North Justice Center, Fullerton
Circumstances of the Case
On Oct. 31, 2017, a large group gathered at CSUF to protest guest speaker Milo Yiannopoulos on campus.
Johnson is accused of possessing pepper spray at the protest outside of the venue, which she attended dressed in tactical gear, including a helmet.
CSUF Police arrested Johnson during the protest and investigated the case.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Devin Campbell, Special Prosecutions Unit
