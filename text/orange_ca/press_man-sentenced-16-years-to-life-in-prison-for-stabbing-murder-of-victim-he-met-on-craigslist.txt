MAN SENTENCED 16 YEARS TO LIFE IN PRISON FOR STABBING-MURDER OF VICTIM HE MET ON CRAIGSLIST
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A man was sentenced 16 years to life in state prison today for the stabbing-murder of a man he met on Craigslist.
Defendant
Charges
Sentence
Adam Anthony Ingala-Whiting, 25, Oceanside
Found guilty by a jury on March 21, 2018, of:
One felony count of second degree murder
Sentencing Enhancement
Personal use of a knife
16 years to life in state prison
Circumstances of the Case
In the early morning hours of April 29, 2014, Ingala-Whiting responded to a Craigslist advertisement posted by the victim soliciting a sexual encounter.
The defendant met 47-year-old Francesco Bonomolo at his home in Mission Viejo.
Ingala-Whiting murdered Bonomolo by stabbing him multiple times while in the victim’s home.
On May 4, 2014, an acquaintance went to Bonomolo’s home and discovered the victim’s body.
The Orange County Sheriff’s Department investigated this case and arrested Ingala-Whiting in San Diego County on May 12, 2014.
Prosecutor: Senior Deputy District Attorney Troy Pino, Homicide Unit
