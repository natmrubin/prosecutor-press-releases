FORMER SECURITY MANAGER OF AMERICAN JUNKIE NIGHTCLUB CHARGED WITH FURNISHING DRUGS TO CLUB PATRONS CAUSING MULTIPLE OVERDOSES
SANTA ANA, Calif. – The former security manager of American Junkie bar and nightclub was charged today with furnishing drugs to club patrons, causing multiple overdoses.

Circumstances of the Case
At the time of the crime, McLaughlin was employed as the security manager for a nightclub in Newport Beach.
On Sept. 18, 2016, the defendant is accused of holding a woman against her will inside his office at the nightclub and supplying her with ecstasy.
Prior to Nov. 18, 2016, the defendant is further accused of furnishing cocaine laced with fentanyl to four club patrons.
At approximately 1:05 a.m on Nov. 18, 2016, the Newport Beach Fire Department and Newport Beach Police Department (NBPD) responded to a call for medical aid for multiple victims at a business in the 2400 block of Newport Boulevard.
Four victims were transported to local hospitals, where one man later died.
On Dec. 8, 2017, NBPD, who investigated this case, arrested the defendant.
Prosecutor: Senior Deputy District Attorney Robert Mestman, Felony Panel
