MAN TO BE ARRAIGNED FOR STABBING THREE MARINES OUTSIDE OF BAR IN SAN CLEMENTE
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A man is scheduled to be arraigned today for stabbing three U.S. Marines outside of a bar in San Clemente.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Alexis Moreno Aguirre, 24, Dana Point
Charged today with the following felony counts:
(3) Assault with a deadly weapon – knife
Sentencing Enhancements
(3) Causing great bodily injury
14 years in state prison
Arraignment
Aug. 7, 2018, time to be determined
Department CJ-1, Central Jail, Santa Ana
Circumstances of the Case
On Aug. 4, 2018, at approximately 1:00 a.m., Moreno Aguirre is accused of engaging in an altercation with three Marines in the parking lot of a bar on El Camino Real in San Clemente.
The defendant is accused of using a knife to stab the victims. John Doe 1 received injuries to his abdomen, John Doe 2 sustained lacerations on his abdomen and throat, and John Doe 3’s abdomen and shoulder were injured.
Moreno Aguirre is accused of fleeing the scene and witnesses called 911. The victims were transported to local hospitals and survived the assault.
Later that morning, the defendant is accused of going to a hospital in Laguna Beach to treat injuries sustained during the incident.
The Orange County Sheriff’s Department investigated this case and arrested Moreno Aguirre at the hospital.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Aaron Van Stralen, Harbor Justice Center
