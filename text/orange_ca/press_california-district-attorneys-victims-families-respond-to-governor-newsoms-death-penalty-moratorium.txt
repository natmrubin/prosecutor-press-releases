CALIFORNIA DISTRICT ATTORNEYS, VICTIMS’ FAMILIES RESPOND TO GOVERNOR NEWSOM’S DEATH PENALTY MORATORIUM
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A coalition of California District Attorneys and family members of murder victims will hold a press conference at 10 a.m. on Thursday, April 11 to respond to the death penalty moratorium imposed by Governor Gavin Newsom last month. Victims’ families will share their stories of losing their loved ones and how the governor’s moratorium has devastated their pursuit of justice.
Death Penalty Moratorium Press Conference
Thursday, April 11, 2019 at 10 a.m.
California District Attorneys Association
921 11th Street, #300
Sacramento, CA 95814
Invited Guests:
Jeannie Budfuloski, sister of homicide victim William Budfuloski, and aunt of homicide victim Michael Budfuloski
Sandy Friend, mother of 8-year-old homicide victim Michael Lyons
Steve and Raquel Herr, parents of homicide victim Sam Herr
Ron Harrington, brother of homicide victim Keith Harrington
Phyllis Loya, mother of homicide victim Pittsburg Police Officer Larry Lasater Jr.
Orange County District Attorney Todd Spitzer
El Dorado County District Attorney Vern Pierson
Riverside County District Attorney Mike Hestrin
Sacramento County District Attorney Anne Marie Schubert
San Luis Obispo County District Attorney Dan Dow
Ventura County District Attorney Greg Totten
