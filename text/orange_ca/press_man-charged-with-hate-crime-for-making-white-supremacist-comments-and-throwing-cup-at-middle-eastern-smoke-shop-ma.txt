MAN CHARGED WITH HATE CRIME FOR MAKING WHITE SUPREMACIST COMMENTS AND THROWING CUP AT MIDDLE EASTERN SMOKE SHOP MANAGER
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A man was charged with a hate crime for making white supremacist comments and throwing a cup at a Middle Eastern smoke shop manager in Anaheim.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Robert Kevin Pressler, 54, transient
Charged on May 4, 2018, with the following:
One felony count of battery – hate crime causing injury
One misdemeanor count of possession of a controlled substance
Seven years in state prison
Pre-trial hearing
May 14, 2018
Department N-3, North Justice Center, Fullerton
Circumstances of the Case
On May 2, 2018, Pressler is accused of standing outside of a smoke shop in Anaheim and repeatedly yelling a phrase associated with white supremacy at the store manager, John Doe, who is Middle Eastern.
As the victim approached the front entrance of the store, the defendant is accused of walking toward John Doe and throwing a full 32-ounce soda cup at him.
The cup hit the victim’s face, which caused a small laceration and initiated an altercation between Pressler and John Doe.
The defendant called the Anaheim Police Department (APD) following the altercation.
Pressler is further accused of possessing methamphetamine at the time of his arrest by APD.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Devin Campbell, Special Prosecutions Unit
