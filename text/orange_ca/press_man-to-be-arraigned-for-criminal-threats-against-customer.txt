MAN TO BE ARRAIGNED FOR CRIMINAL THREATS AGAINST CUSTOMER
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A man is scheduled to be arraigned tomorrow for criminal threats against a customer.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Mitchell Ralph Todd, 51, Huntington Beach
Charged on Dec. 6, 2018, with the following:
One felony count of criminal threats
Three years in state prison
Arraignment
Dec. 28, 2018 at 8:30 a.m.
Department H-7, Harbor Justice Center, Newport Beach
Circumstances of the Case
At the time of the crimes, Todd was the owner of OC Doves and was hired by John Doe to release doves at a funeral service.
On Nov. 10, 2018, the defendant is accused of getting into a disagreement with the victim and not rendering services. Todd is accused of requesting payment from John Doe.
On Nov. 11, 2018, the defendant is accused of leaving the victim a series of threatening voicemails and further demanding payment.
On the last voicemail left for John Doe, Todd is accused of recording the sound of a handgun slide being pulled back and released as if chambering a round.
During a search of Todd’s house, Laguna Beach Police Department (LBPD), who investigated this case, located 57 firearms, nearly 30,000 rounds of ammunition, over 100 magazines, a practice bomb, several body armors, swords, and Nazi paraphernalia.
LBPD arrested the defendant on an arrest warrant on Dec. 13, 2018.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Laila Nikaien, Special Prosecutions Unit
