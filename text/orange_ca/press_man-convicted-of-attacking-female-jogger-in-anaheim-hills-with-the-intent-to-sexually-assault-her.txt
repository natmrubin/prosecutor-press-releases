MAN CONVICTED OF ATTACKING FEMALE JOGGER IN ANAHEIM HILLS WITH THE INTENT TO SEXUALLY ASSAULT HER
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A man was convicted today of attacking a woman on a jogging trail with the intent to sexually assault her.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Mario Orlando Montano, 59, Anaheim
Found guilty by a jury today of the following felony counts:
Assault with the intent to commit a sexual offense
Assault with force likely to produce great bodily injury
Sentencing Enhancements Found True
Great bodily injury to a sexual assault victim
Great bodily injury
11 years in state prison
Sentencing
Aug. 4, 2017, 9:00 a.m.
Department C-36, Central Justice Center, Santa Ana
Circumstances of the Case
On the afternoon on Dec. 10, 2013, 24-year-old Jane Doe was jogging on a trail in a reservoir in Anaheim Hills.
When she slowed to a walk, Montano jogged up to the victim and tried to engage her in conversation, then tackled Jane Doe to the ground.
The defendant climbed on top of her as she kicked and struggled to get away.
When the victim began to scream, Montano covered her mouth. The victim’s tooth was knocked out in the struggle.
The defendant reached for his pants with the intent to commit a sexual assault, but Jane Doe was able to kick him and escape.
Jane Doe ran to a nearby home and called 911.
The Anaheim Police Department (APD) immediately responded and located the defendant on the trail.
APD investigated this case.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Harris Siddiq, Felony Panel
