LAW ENFORCEMENT SEEKING POTENTIAL ADDITIONAL VICTIMS OF MAN CHARGED WITH FORCIBLY RAPING STRANGER IN DOWNTOWN HUNTINGTON BEACH
SANTA ANA, Calif. – Law enforcement is seeking potential additional victims of a man charged yesterday with forcibly raping a woman he did not know in downtown Huntington Beach.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Kyle Patrick Ryan, 34, Santa Ana
Charged on May 15, 2018, with the following felony counts:
Forcible rape
Rape by use of drugs
Sodomy by force
Sodomy by intoxicating substance
Forcible oral copulation
Oral copulation by intoxicating substance
False imprisonment by menace, violence, fraud, and deceit
61 years and four months in state prison
Lifetime sex offender registration pursuant to penal code 290
Continued arraignment
June 1, 2018, 10:00 a.m.
Department CJ-1, Central Jail, Santa Ana
Circumstances of the Case
On the night of May 5, 2018, Ryan is accused of driving in downtown Huntington Beach and contacting 50-year-old Jane Doe. The defendant and victim were strangers.
Ryan is accused of forcibly raping and sexually assaulting the victim, who was intoxicated, until she was able to escape.
The defendant is further accused of fleeing the scene in his vehicle.
Jane Doe went to a nearby residence for help, and the Huntington Beach Police Department (HBPD) responded and investigated this case.
HBPD identified Ryan and arrested the defendant in Santa Ana on May 11, 2018.
Law enforcement believe there may be additional victims and are asking anyone with information regarding the crime to call the Huntington Beach Police Department Hotline at 714-375-5066, or contact Orange County District Attorney’s Office Supervising Investigator Eric Wiseman at 714-347-8794.
Prosecutor: Senior Deputy District Attorney Bobby Taghavi, Sexual Assault Unit
