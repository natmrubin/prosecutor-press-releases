MEDIA ADVISORY
WHO: Laguna Hills City Council Mayor L. Allan Songstad, Jr., Mayor pro tem Melody Carruth, and Council Members Joel Lautenschleger, Barbara Kogerman, and Randal Bressette
WHAT: Voted unanimously yesterday at their City Council meeting to have a first reading on Nov. 22, 2011, of a City Ordinance to create a child safety zone in city parks to further protect children from registered sex offenders. The Council directed the City Attorney and staff to draft a City Ordinance to be modeled after the County Ordinance establishing city parks as “Child Safety Zones,” where registered sex offenders are prohibited from entering without written permission from the Orange County Sheriff or her designee.
The County Ordinance, proposed by Orange County District Attorney Tony Rackauckas and Supervisor Shawn Nelson (Fourth District), was passed unanimously by the Orange County Board of Supervisors on April 5, 2011. The County Ordinance makes it a misdemeanor for registered sex offenders to enter County recreational areas where children regularly gather.
To read the full County Ordinance, please select the April 5, 2011, press release titled “Orange County Board of Supervisors Unanimously Votes to Adopt New Law to Keep Registered Sex Offenders Out of Parks, Harbors, Beaches and Playgrounds,” under Press Releases/Media Advisories at
www.orangecountyda.com
