OCDA RACKAUCKAS ANNOUNCES CONSUMER PROTECTION SETTLEMENT OVER UNINTENDED ACCELERATION
SANTA
ANA,
CALIFORNIA
–
Throughout 2009 and 2010, the Toyota Motor Corporation issued
recalls
affecting nearly 6 million vehicles due to the risk of unintended acceleration caused by floor
mat
and “sticky” gas pedal
issues.
Today Orange County District Attorney
(OCDA)
Tony Rackauckas announced a multi-million
dollar
settlement with Toyota and its related North American entities over allegations the
company
concealed safety issues related to unintended acceleration in violation of California
consumer
protection
laws.
Toyota will pay $16 million to settle consumer protection claims brought by the OCDA
.
