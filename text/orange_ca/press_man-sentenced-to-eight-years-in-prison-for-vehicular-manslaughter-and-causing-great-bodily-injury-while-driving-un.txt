MAN SENTENCED TO EIGHT YEARS IN PRISON FOR VEHICULAR MANSLAUGHTER AND CAUSING GREAT BODILY INJURY WHILE DRIVING UNDER THE INFLUENCE OF ALCOHOL
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A man was sentenced yesterday to eight years in state prison for vehicular manslaughter and causing great bodily injury while driving under the influence of alcohol.
Defendant
Charges
Sentence
Steven Gerard Cruz, 34, Cerritos
Found guilty by a jury Aug. 28, 2018 on the following felony counts:
Gross vehicular manslaughter while intoxicated
Driving under the influence of alcohol causing injury
Driving with blood alcohol .08 percent or more causing bodily injury
Sentencing Enhancements
:
(2) Great bodily injury
Eight years and eight months in state prison
Circumstances of the Case
On May 10, 2015, Cruz drove east on Orangethorpe Avenue in Buena Park while under the influence of alcohol with a blood alcohol content of .11 percent.
At an intersection, the defendant accelerated through a yellow light and crashed into the driver’s side of a Toyota Avalon.
John Doe, the driver of the Toyota Avalon, was transported by ambulance to UCI Medical Center where he later died of his injuries.
Cruz also caused great bodily injury to Jane Doe 1 and Jane Doe 2, who were John Doe’s passengers.
Buena Park Police Department (BPPD) investigated this case.
At the sentencing, the victim’s daughter read impact statements. She stated, in part, “This is the most painful part for me and my mom, missing my dad…My parents were going to be celebrating their 60
th
wedding anniversary only four days after that but instead, my mom was in the hospital and my dad was in the morgue at the coroner’s office.”
Prosecutor: Senior Deputy District Attorney Daniel Feldman, Homicide Unit
