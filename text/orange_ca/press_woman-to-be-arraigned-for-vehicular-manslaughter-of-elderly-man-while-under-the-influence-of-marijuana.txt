WOMAN TO BE ARRAIGNED FOR VEHICULAR MANSLAUGHTER OF
ELDERLY MAN WHILE UNDER THE INFLUENCE OF MARIJUANA
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A woman will be arraigned tomorrow for the vehicular manslaughter of an elderly man while she was under the influence of marijuana.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Aliza Keiley Giglio, 24, Stanton
Charged on Oct. 3, 2018 with the following:
One felony count of gross vehicular manslaughter while intoxicated
10 years in state prison
Arraignment
Oct. 19, 2018 at 10:00 a.m.
Dept. CJ-1, Central Jail, Santa Ana
Circumstances of the Case
On Aug. 6, 2017, at approximately 8:20 a.m., 81-year-old Clark Adams was stopped at a red light on Santiago Canyon Road in Orange.
Giglio is accused of driving under the influence of marijuana, approaching the intersection in her sedan without slowing or stopping for the red light, and colliding with the rear end of Adams’ vehicle.
The defendant is accused of creating an impact that caused the victim’s vehicle to go across the intersection, fatally injuring Adams, who was pronounced dead at a hospital later that day.
Giglio is accused of displaying objective signs of intoxication including erratic behavior and dilated pupils. Blood test results showed levels of THC in the defendant’s system consistent with recently smoking marijuana.
Orange Police Department (OPD) investigated this case and arrested Giglio in Anaheim on Oct. 17, 2018.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Brian Orue, Homicide Unit
