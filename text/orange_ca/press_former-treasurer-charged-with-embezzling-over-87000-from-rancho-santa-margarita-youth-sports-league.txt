FORMER TREASURER CHARGED WITH EMBEZZLING OVER $87,000 FROM RANCHO SANTA MARGARITA YOUTH SPORTS LEAGUE
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A former treasurer was charged with embezzling more than $87,000 from a Rancho Santa Margarita youth sports league.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Joshua Paul Nowack, 43, Trabuco Canyon
Charged on Sept. 12, 2018 with the following felony counts:
(18) Embezzlement by fiduciary of trust
Sentencing Enhancement:
Property damage over $65,000
15 years and four months in Orange County jail
Arraignment
Sept. 14, 2018, 10:00 a.m.
Department CJ-1, Central Jail, Santa Ana
Circumstances of the Case
At the time of the crime, Nowack was the treasurer of the Tijeras Creek Little League in Rancho Santa Margarita.
Between January 2014 and September 2017, the defendant is accused of embezzling more than $87,500 from the youth sports league by transferring funds from the league to personal bank accounts.
In October 2017, Nowack’s successor reviewed the League’s financial records and noticed suspicious withdrawals and deposits.
The embezzlement was reported to the Orange County Sheriff’s Department (OCSD), who investigated this case.
Nowack was arrested by OCSD on Sept. 13, 2018.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Nicholas Miller, Major Fraud Unit
