MAN CONVICTED OF HAVING UNLAWFUL SEX AND SMOKING MARIJUANA WITH
12-YEAR-OLD GIRL
IN HIS CAR
SANTA ANA – A 32-year-old man was convicted today of having unlawful sex and smoking marijuana with a 12-year-old girl in his car at 2:00 a.m. after meeting her for the first time earlier that day.
Neftali Pena Procopio, Santa Ana, pleaded guilty to the court to two felony counts of lewd acts on a child under 14, one felony count of furnishing marijuana to a minor, and sentencing enhancements for substantial sexual conduct with a child. He is also charged with.
Procopio faces a maximum sentence of 11 years and eight months in state prison at his sentencing April 1, 2011, at 9:00 a.m. in Department C-55, Central Justice Center, Santa Ana.
On the afternoon of Aug. 16, 2010, Procopio met 12-year-old Jane Doe for the first time while he was playing basketball on the campus of Santa Ana High School. The victim is not a student at the high school. The defendant arranged for the victim to sneak out of her home later that night.
Later that night, Procopio picked Jane Doe up near her home, drove her to the 300 block of South Ross Street in Santa Ana, and parked his car. The defendant gave Jane Doe marijuana and smoked it with her. He then had unlawful sex with the victim in his car and tossed the used condom outside.
At approximately 2:00 a.m. on Aug. 17, 2010, an officer from the Santa Ana Police Department observed the defendant’s parked car with fogged windows. The officer approached the vehicle to make contact with the occupants. Procopio was found inside the car with Jane Doe when the officer approached.
The defendant was arrested at the scene and the victim was taken home by SAPD to her parents, who were unaware that she had left the house. The discarded condom was recovered by officers.
Senior Deputy District Attorney Jana Hoffmann of the Sexual Assault Unit is prosecuting this case.
