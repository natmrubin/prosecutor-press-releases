Date: July 17, 2018
HOMELESS MAN CHARGED WITH STARTING MULTIPLE FIRES
ALONG SANTIAGO CREEK
SANTA ANA, Calif. – A homeless man was charged today with starting multiple fires along Santiago Creek within the past month.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Gustavo Abarca Garcia, 26
Charged with the following felony counts:
(7) Arson of forest
Arson of another’s property
Vandalism
15 years and three months in state prison
Pre-trial hearing
July 25, 2018, 9:00 a.m.
Department C-55, Central Justice Center, Santa Ana
Circumstances of the Case
In July 2018, Garcia is accused of starting seven fires in forest and vegetation areas of Santiago Creek in Santa Ana and Orange.
On July 13, 2018, the defendant is accused of setting fire to a fence on River Lane in Santa Ana. The fire spread to the home’s backyard and burned the homeowners’ patio furniture, vegetation, and trampoline, totaling over $20,000 of damage.
The Santa Ana Police Department (SAPD), Orange Police Department, and Orange County Fire Authority investigated this case. SAPD officers located and arrested Garcia near the scene.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Claudia Alvarez, Special Prosecutions Unit
