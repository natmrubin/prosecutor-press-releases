OFF-DUTY POLICE OFFICER ARRAIGNED FOR DUI AFTER FALLING ASLEEP AT THE WHEEL ON 55 FREEWAY
SANTA ANA, Calif. – An off-duty Garden Grove Police Department (GGPD) officer was arraigned yesterday for driving under the influence (DUI) after falling asleep at the wheel on the 55 freeway.
Defendant
Charges
Maximum Sentence
Court Date
Jared Russell Doyle, 32
Charged on July 3, 2018, with the following misdemeanor counts:
Driving under the influence of alcohol
Driving with blood alcohol content (BAC) .08 percent or more
Six months in county jail
Pre-trial Hearing
Sept. 5, 2018, 8:30 a.m.
Department C-60, Central Justice Center, Santa Ana
Circumstances of the Case
At the time of the crime, Doyle was employed as a police officer with GGPD and was off-duty.
On May 27, 2018, at approximately 8:00 a.m., Doyle is accused of driving on the 55 freeway while under the influence of alcohol and weaving between lanes.
The defendant is accused of falling asleep at the wheel and coming to a stop in the middle of the freeway.
Witnesses called 911 and California Highway Patrol (CHP) officers responded to the scene.
CHP arrested Doyle, who is accused of having a blood alcohol content of 0.16 percent.
Prosecutor: Deputy District Attorney Jeffrey Kirk, Special Prosecutions Unit
