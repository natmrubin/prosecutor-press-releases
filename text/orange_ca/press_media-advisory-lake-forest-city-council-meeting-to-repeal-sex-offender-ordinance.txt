December 18, 2012
Susan Kang Schroeder
Chief of Staff
Office: 714-347-8408
Cell: 714-292-2718
Farrah Emami

MEDIA ADVISORY
WHO: Orange County District Attorney’s Chief of Staff Susan Kang Schroeder
WHAT: Will attend the Lake Forest City Council meeting tonight in support of keeping a City Ordinance to maintain a child safety zone in city parks to further protect children from registered sex offenders. This will be the second meeting to approve the repeal of the ordinance. The City Ordinance that was passed last year is similar to the County Ordinance proposed by District Attorney Rackauckas and Supervisor Shawn Nelson (Fourth District) and passed unanimously by the Orange County Board of Supervisors on April 5, 2011. In the past year, the cities of
Costa Mesa, Huntington Beach, Irvine, Laguna Hills, Lake Forest, La Habra, Los Alamitos, Mission Viejo, Rancho Santa Margarita, Santa Ana, Seal Beach, Westminster, Yorba Linda, Fountain Valley, and Anaheim
passed similar ordinances, and other cities are currently in the process of developing a similar ordinance.
WHEN: Tonight, Tuesday, Dec. 18, 2012, at 7:00 p.m.
WHERE: Council Chambers in City Hall, 25550 Commercentre Drive, Lake Forest
To read the full County Ordinance, please select the April 5, 2011, press release titled “Orange County Board of Supervisors Unanimously Votes to Adopt New Law to Keep Registered Sex Offenders Out of Parks, Harbors, Beaches and Playgrounds,” under Press Releases/Media Advisories at
www.orangecountyda.com
To read the agenda item, please select “Agenda” under City Services from the homepage at
www.lakeforestca.gov
and select the City Council Agenda on the pull down menu and select current agendas and click on the agenda for Dec. 18, 2012.
