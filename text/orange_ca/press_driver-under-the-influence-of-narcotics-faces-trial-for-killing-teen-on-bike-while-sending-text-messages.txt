DRIVER UNDER THE INFLUENCE OF NARCOTICS FACES TRIAL FOR KILLING TEEN ON BIKE WHILE SENDING TEXT MESSAGES
SANTA ANA – A driver under the influence of narcotics faces trial tomorrow for killing a 14-year-old on a bike by crashing into the victim while text messaging behind the wheel. Jeffrey Francis Woods, 22, Huntington Beach, was indicted by the Grand Jury on Nov. 10, 2008, on one felony count of vehicular manslaughter by unlawful act with gross negligence while intoxicated and one felony count of driving under the influence causing bodily injury. He faces a maximum sentence of 10 years in state prison if convicted. Opening statements are expected to begin tomorrow, Thursday, Oct. 8, 2009, at 9:30 a.m. in Department C-39, Central Justice Center, Santa Ana.
Shortly after noon on Aug. 29, 2007, Woods is accused of driving in a residential area in his pick-up truck eastbound on Indianapolis Avenue in Huntington Beach. He is accused of driving
erratically and following too close to other vehicles while under the influence of controlled substances, Vicodin and Xanax. Woods is accused of sending text messages and being distracted while driving. He is accused of suddenly swerving over several lanes into oncoming traffic.
Woods is accused of crossing into the bike lane on the wrong side of the road in his truck and crashing into 14-year-old Daniel Oates, who was riding his bicycle with a friend. The victim was thrown 150 feet from his bicycle and landed in a traffic lane. Oates died from the impact of the crash caused by Woods. The defendant is accused of then crashing into a palm tree, driving through a wall, and coming to a stop in the backyard of a nearby home.
Deputy District Attorney Susan Price of the Homicide Unit is prosecuting this case.
